
# Executive Summary

## Objective
The goal of this project was to identify high-value credit card customers with strong potential for cross-selling additional financial products.

---

## Key Findings

- Analyzed more than 10,000 customer records
- Built automated ETL pipelines for financial customer analytics
- Created engagement scoring framework using transaction activity
- Segmented customers into high, medium, and low engagement tiers
- Built Logistic Regression and XGBoost propensity models
- Identified high-value cross-sell customer opportunities

---

## Business Insights

### High Engagement Segment
Customers with:
- High transaction counts
- Higher spend velocity
- Multiple financial relationships

showed the strongest likelihood of adopting additional financial products.

### Cross-Sell Opportunity
Approximately 25–40% of customers demonstrated high cross-sell potential based on engagement behavior and relationship depth.

### Dormancy Risk
Low transaction frequency and lower engagement scores strongly correlated with dormant behavior.

---

## Recommendations

1. Target high-engagement customers with premium lending or savings products
2. Build personalized marketing campaigns for medium-tier customers
3. Launch retention campaigns for dormant customer cohorts
4. Use propensity scores for prioritizing relationship manager outreach

---

## Technical Outcomes

- ETL automation completed
- SQLite analytics layer implemented
- Customer segmentation using KMeans clustering
- Propensity modeling with Logistic Regression and XGBoost
- Business KPI dashboard generated
